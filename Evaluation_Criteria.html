Ranking Framework

Evaluation criteria and grading spread.

Each group receives points for readability, consistency, translation quality, and localization quality. Picture quality is tracked separately as an x-factor and does not count toward the 100-point overall grade.

Core Criteria

The four scored categories add up to 100 possible points.

Criterion Points Description
Readability 0-40p How easy it is to understand the translation while reading normally.
Consistency 0-20p How stable the quality is across chapters, series, and time.
Translation Quality 0-30p How good the wording, grammar, and phrasing are when judged directly.
Localization Quality 0-10p How good the localization is.
Picture Quality X-factor How clear the panels are. Tracked separately from the overall point total.

Uncertain Grades

Use a question mark only after broad grades: S?, A?, B?, C?, D?, or F?.

Do not use A+? or A-?.

Design Notes

  • S+ is an intentionally near-unreachable ceiling for practical perfection.
  • S-tier is compressed so only exceptional groups qualify.
  • Consistency is stricter because quality stability leaves less room for interpretation.
  • Localization is more flexible because standards vary by language, region, and audience.
